9 Most Devastating Earthquakes In History by Monica Arias

Ranking Location Year Estimated death toll Earthquake magnitude Additional informationn
1 Shaanxi, China 1556 830,000 8 Over 97 Chinese counties hit, with a 520-mile wide area devastated. Up to 60% population loss in some counties due to collapsed loess cave settlements.
2 Port-au-Prince, Haiti 2010 316,000 7 The death toll remains contested. The NGDC of NOAA adopts the figure reported by the Haitian government. Other sources propose a lower count of 220,000, which would rank this event 7th.
3 Antakya, Turkey 115 260,000 7.5 Antioch and nearby areas, including Apamea, suffered severe damage. Beirut also sustained significant destruction. A local tsunami caused coastal damage in Lebanon.
4 Antakya, Turkey 525 250,000 7 The Byzantine Empire area faced severe damage from the earthquake, impacting many buildings. Subsequent fires, fueled by strong winds, also caused significant destruction.
5 Tangshan, China 1976 242,769 7.5 The earthquake risk was significantly underestimated, leading to buildings being constructed without seismic considerations. Roughly 85% of buildings collapsed, primarily unreinforced brick structures, contributing to a high death toll in Tangshan.
6 Gyzndzha, Azerbaijan 1139 230,000 Unknown Known as the Ganja earthquake, with limited documentation on specific details.
7 Sumatra, Indonesia 2004 227,899 9.1 Indian Ocean earthquake off Sumatra caused massive tsunamis (15-30 meters high), affecting 14 countries. Indonesia, Sri Lanka, India, and Thailand were hardest hit, with no tsunami warning system in place.
8 Damghan, Iran 856 200,000 7.9 The damage area spanned approximately 220 miles, possibly leading to the abandonment of the ancient city of Šahr-e Qumis.
9 Dvin, Armenia 893 150,000 Unknown Dvin city was devastated, with most buildings, walls, and palaces collapsing, leaving only around 100 standing. It was then seized and repurposed as a military base by Muhammad ibn Abi'l-Saj, the Sajid emir of Adharbayjan.
10 Tokyo, Japan 1923 142,807 7.9 Over 50% of brick buildings and 10% of reinforced structures collapsed. A tsunami reaching up to 12m was triggered. Large fires, along with a tornado, spread rapidly.
